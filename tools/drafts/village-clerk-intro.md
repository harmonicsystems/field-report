**Subject:** Kinderhook Field Report — a civic legibility project, and a small request

---

Dear [Clerk's name],

My name is David Nyman. I am a resident of Kinderhook and the principal of
Harmonic Systems, a small practice based in the village. I'm writing to
introduce a project I've been building as a neighbor and to ask the village's
guidance on a detail of how it reads your site.

The project is called the **Kinderhook Field Report**. It's a structured-data
audit of how our village appears to the web that language models, search
assistants, and the next generation of discovery tools actually use. In plain
terms: when someone asks an AI assistant *"where can I get produce in the
Hudson Valley on Saturday?"*, the assistant can only recommend the
institutions whose information it can structurally read. A lot of what makes
Kinderhook Kinderhook — the farmers' market, the volunteer-run groups, the
small shops and historic sites — is invisible to that layer of the internet
today. The Field Report is my attempt to make it visible, under our own
editorial control, linked back to authoritative sources like the village
site rather than replacing them.

You can see the work in progress at
**https://fieldreports.harmonic-systems.org**. Source code and the full
editorial record are public at https://github.com/harmonicsystems/field-report.

**The small request.** The village's business directory at
villageofkinderhook.org/community/business_directory.php is an excellent
piece of civic data — clean categories, stable records. I've written a tool
that reads that page once a day and turns it into a structured JSON snapshot
(dated, diffable, source-attributed to your pages) so that the Field Report
can cite it correctly. I wanted to let you know I'm doing that, and to ask:

1. Is this acceptable to the village? If it is not, I will stop
   immediately. Nothing in the project is worth doing against the village's
   preference.
2. If it *is* acceptable — would the village be open, at some point, to
   publishing a small machine-readable feed (an iCal file for the calendar,
   or a JSON export of the business directory) that I could consume
   directly? It would save me from parsing the page, and more importantly it
   would make the same information available to every other assistant,
   search engine, and civic project that might want it. I'd be glad to help
   scope or build that with your IT contact at no cost; my professional
   background is in structured communication.

Either answer is a good answer. I'll wait to hear from you before publishing
anything derived from the directory beyond what's already on the public page.

Thank you for the work you do for the village.

Warmly,

David Nyman
