## § 130-3 Designation of districts (cited verbatim)

[Amended 2-15-1979 by L.L. No. 1-1979; 2-14-1996 by L.L. No. 1-1996;
6-29-2000 by L.L. No. 4-2000.]

> To achieve the purposes of the Village of Kinderhook Code and to
> implement the Comprehensive Plan, the Village of Kinderhook hereby
> establishes the following seven districts and two overlay zones [...]

### A. Base zoning districts

| Code | Name | Purpose (paraphrased) | Density |
|------|------|-----------------------|---------|
| `F-1`  | 100-Year Flood Plain District | Preserve floodplain function | — |
| `A`    | Agricultural District | Protect sensitive lands; agriculture and open space | 1 unit / 120,000 sf |
| `AR`   | Agricultural/Residential | Moderate-density with mandatory conservation subdivisions | 1 unit / 120,000 sf overall |
| `R-1`  | Residential | Lower-density residential | 1 unit / 40,000 sf |
| `R`    | Residential | Higher-density consistent with traditional/historical core | 1 unit / 20,000 sf |
| `B-1`  | Business | Focal point for commercial; pedestrian Village character | — |
| `B-2`  | Business | Auto-oriented or larger-lot commercial (concentrated node) | — |

### B. Overlay zones

| Code | Name | Purpose |
|------|------|---------|
| `F-2` | 500-Year Flood Plain Overlay | Preserve floodplain function |
| **`H`**  | **Historic Preservation District** | Preserve historic and architectural character; regulate exterior alteration, new construction, restoration, demolition. **This is the overlay whose boundary appears on the Zoning Map and which § 75-5 references.** |
