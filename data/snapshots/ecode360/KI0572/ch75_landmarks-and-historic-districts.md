# Chapter 75 — Landmarks and Historic Districts (excerpts we cite)

## § 75-1 Purpose (cited verbatim opening)

> It is hereby declared as a matter of public policy that the protection,
> enhancement and perpetuation of landmarks and historic districts is
> necessary to promote the economic, cultural, educational, and general
> welfare of the public.

Stated intents (paraphrased): protect Kinderhook's historic, architectural,
and cultural heritage; foster civic pride; protect attractiveness to
residents, businesses, and visitors; support orderly growth.

## § 75-3 Historic Preservation Commission (key facts cited)

- Created by this section as the **Village of Kinderhook Historic
  Preservation Commission**.
- Five members appointed by the Village Board, plus an optional alternate.
- Members must demonstrate significant interest in historic preservation
  and architecture; at least three should be residents and/or property
  owners in the Historic District where possible.
- Five-year staggered terms.
- Powers include: adopt criteria for landmarks and historic districts,
  formulate design guidelines, conduct surveys, recommend designations,
  approve/deny **certificates of appropriateness**, coordinate with the
  Planning Board and ZBA.
- Quorum: three members.
- Amended 3-9-2022 by L.L. No. 1-2022.

## § 75-4 Designation of landmarks or historic districts (criteria summary)

The Trustees designate landmarks and create or modify historic districts on
recommendation of the HPC. A property may be designated a landmark if it
(among other criteria):

1. Possesses special historic, aesthetic, cultural, political, economic,
   or social value.
2. Is identified with historic personages.
3. Embodies the distinguishing characteristics of an architectural style.
4. Is the work of a designer whose work has significantly influenced an era.
5. Represents an established and familiar visual feature of the neighborhood.
6. Embodies distinctive characteristics of a type, period, or method of
   construction, or possesses high artistic value.

## § 75-5 Historic Preservation District and Map (BOUNDARY DEFINITION)

This is the load-bearing section for our project. Cited verbatim:

> A. The Historic Preservation District shall be the existing Historic
> Preservation Overlay District Boundary depicted on the Zoning Map in
> Chapter 130, Zoning, of the Code of the Village of Kinderhook, and any
> other districts or landmarks designated by the Trustees. District
> boundaries shall follow the lot lines of designated historic structures
> as surveyed and symbolically depicted on Tax Maps that form the perimeter
> of the district as shown on the Zoning Map.

> B. The Historic Preservation Map shall show the location(s) and
> boundaries of landmarks and historic districts, and it shall be on file
> with the Village Clerk. Said map shall be adopted and declared a part of
> this chapter. The Historic Preservation Map shall henceforth be
> considered separate from Chapter 130, Zoning, and the Zoning Map.

> C. Where uncertainty exists with respect to the boundaries of any of the
> aforesaid districts shown on the Historic Preservation Map [...] [rules
> for centerlines, lot lines, and parallel boundaries follow].

### Implication for kinderhook.json

- The HD boundary is **graphical**, not metes-and-bounds. Two authoritative
  copies exist: (a) the Zoning Map attached to Ch. 130, and (b) the
  separate Historic Preservation Map on file with the Village Clerk.
- The boundary is defined by **tax-map lot lines** of designated structures,
  not by street centerlines.
- For our overlay we will need either the Zoning Map PDF (look for
  ecode360 attachment to Ch. 130) or to obtain the Historic Preservation
  Map from the Village Clerk and digitize the parcel set.
- The locally-effective HD boundary is what governs property owners and
  is what residents need to know. The National Register HD (NRHP) is a
  separate, federal listing — useful for the map but **not authoritative
  for "is this property regulated as historic."** We should distinguish
  the two clearly in the published overlay.
